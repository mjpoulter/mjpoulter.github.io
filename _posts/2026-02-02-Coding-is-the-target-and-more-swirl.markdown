We continue to see a focus on software development as the pathfinder use case for GenAI and in particular the big labs, eg OpenAI releasing codex desktop app, and taking those capabilities laterally to do activities in general work automation that are extrapolated from software development. Adoption is still tough given the rate of change, and the complexity of safely implmenting agents, skills, mcp servers, but nonetheless the value is clear, the path forward evolving, but clearly a revenue opportunity and fact is starting to replace hype. There was a good post by Sarah Guo on that topic this week on linkedin.

On the other hand, the hype and financing swirl continues. This week we have all the stories about how much Nvidia will actually invest in Openai, SpaceX acquiring xAI and oracle raising debt and capital. Feels like the big suction from AI implemention for capital continues, but maybe folks are realizing now that they need to be more thoughtful? We'll see. I fear accidents ahead, and often the general public suffers as the victim more than the drivers in these sorts of situations.
