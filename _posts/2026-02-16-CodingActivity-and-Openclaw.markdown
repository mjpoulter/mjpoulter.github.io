Its been a very busy couple of weeks. I fell into the trap of waiting for the news to settle down last week before writing and of course it never did.

Anthropic released Opus 4.6, which had the ability to leverage "agent teams" all of which improved claude code and any other tools using Claude. They also introduced Claude Cowork a more generalized agentic coworker, based on Claude code, which introduced some panic at microsoft.

OpenAI released GPT-5.3-codex and codex-spark (running on celebras) and a codex desktop app for mac, all of which shows their renewed focus on competing with anthropic in the software development space. All good for the consumers, but a little difficult to keep up with.

Not to be outdown google released gemini 3 deep think which has impressive capabilities also.

So great progress, particularly for software development capabilities with all the big players making significant improvements.

Having made note of all that, i now have to pivot a little and acknowledge the phenomenon that is Openclaw, which culminated yesterday in the announcement that the originator Peter Steinberger is joining OpenAI (who reportedly won a competition with Meta to secure his services). Openclaw the opensource will move to a foundation and remain open and Peter will turn his attention and clear vision to the future of agents for OpenAI. If you listen to him you can see he has a very clear vision of how to interact with the LLMs and the Agents from the labs and how to utilize those capabilities to build useful tools. He also seems to have developed a very good process for utilizing the tools for building software. Some very pragmatic approaches, more measured than the gastown type advocates, but leveraging the agents to the fullest. He was on a couple of podcasts last week, pragmatic engineer and lex fridman, well worth a listen if you get time.

So where does that leave us. Software development continues to get most of the attention, because its proving the stickiest path to revenue. The gap from the possible to the actual for most enterprises seems to be actually growing wider. And then the move into more general agentic leverage for general office tasks and particular workstreams (goldman sachs announced they are heavily leveraging claude to offload work in trades processing etc). None of this is slowing down, making it hard for many to keep up.
