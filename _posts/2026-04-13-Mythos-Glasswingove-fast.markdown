Another fascinating week in the world of AI related to software. Anthropic have revealed that their new model (codenamed Mythos) is frighteningly capable at finding and exploiting security vulnerabilities. In a move that highlights their emphasis on safety, Anthropic has decided to withhold the model from public availability until the risks can be better evaluated. As part of that work they have pulled together a set of interested parties (cloud providers, major financials, security vendors,etc) to work together to full evaluate the risks and mitigate against them before the model is released. The expectation is that other models will reach the same capability in the not too distant future and so better to guard against the impacts now in an explicit way, rather than ad hoc fight against the cyber attackers as they learn to leverage the models capabilities.
Some have accused Anthropic of virtue signalling, especially on the back of their fight with the department of war (1-1 currently in that conflict), but it feels in keeping with their ethics. And its interesting that OpenAI having long dismissed them, is not making public statements about why they are better positioned (eg access to compute), though most folks would still say that the Anthropic approach is more responsible in that aspect also.

Also interesting to see that OpenAI are now lauding their relationship with AWS as a key part of their business strategy, in that it gives them better entre to the enterprise, via running in Bedrock, and more compute capacity than was coming their way from Microsoft. It seems Microsoft are now responding to the compute shortages as are Anthropic and so the push for more gigawatts continues a pace. Though also interesting that OpenAI lost the folks responsible for the stargate/datacenter build out program last week as they pull in their committments to get more focused ahead of them releasing more finanical details in concert with their desire to go public.

The space is certainly not dull, and if Mythos has the software engineering capabilities the model card represents, then the move to genAI tools generating most code will only accelerate.

On an orthogonal note it was interesting to see Gitbutler get funding. I agree with Scott Chacon (the founder and ex github founding engineer) that Git as currently implemented in github and gitlab needs an uplift to operate well in the world of agentic ai code creation and review. Looking forward to seeing how that all evolves.
