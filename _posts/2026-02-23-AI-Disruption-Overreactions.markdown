This past week was marked by massive corrections to stock market valuations of SaaS, PaaS, Security and Software modernization vendors and folks took each analyst note or foundation model company product announcement. Basically lots of overreactions. Its not that the technology isnt disruptive, its just that folks arent really understanding the nature of the disruption, or the pace at which enterprises will change their behavior. I think we'll see the disruption play out, but over a much longer timeline than the market is forecasting and many of these companies have a chance to pivot and adapt before they are completely disrupted. Maybe folks will start to understand this as the software apocalypse doesnt actually happen overnight and winners and losers become clearer and then we'll get less market whiplash. We'll see.

I do think that as the time and people cost of producing software is reduced the focus will shift to token costs given overall costs are what matters. As that cost drops you'll see more adoption for routine usage and then the adoption will accelerate and we'll see more direct development rather than adoption of some products. But ultimately most enterprises dont want to rebuild an HR system or a General Ledger, those things are not core to revenue generation. And so thats where the enterprises will focus. Revenue generating opportunties and assume their suppliers will take advantage of the same AI capabilites to improve or reduce the costs of their products. And if the current incumbents don't they will get replaced. But not by direct use of GenAI but by new companies benefitting from GenAI to build a better product.

More to come im sure.
