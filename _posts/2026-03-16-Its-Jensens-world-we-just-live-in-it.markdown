The current state of things very much reminds me of the days when Intel was the 800 pound gorilla of the tech world. Its very much the case that everything now rotates around Nvidia. Jensen Huang has taken on the Bill Gates, Steve Jobs mantle. Andy Grove was much quieter not wanting to draw attention to the Intel near monopoly. Jensen seems not to care about that, probably because of the current regulatory climate, but he probably needs to be careful as the regulatory winds can quickly change.

The announcements at GTC were pretty routine, apart from the Groq related pieces. Without any admission that GPUs are not ideal for inference, Jensen went through how the Groq IP is going to be productized. It is quite a clever design to gang together 256 chips into a single cluster, aggregating the sram on each chip to produce a bigger pool of memory. It appears to be a performant design, that will in fact improve inference performance and costs, and no doubt the future evolution of that technology inside Nvidia will further improve their position.

The Anthropic saga continues with a large number of amicus briefs in support of their position in their court case suing the DOW for the supply chain risk notification. Ive got to think that they have a winning argument but that doesnt mean they come out winners in the end. We'll see.

Microsoft shuffle their AI related ranks again, with Satya putting more of the Copilot related work directly under his control. He clearly thinks they are in danger of falling behind. Also moving Mustafa Suleyman to be focused on building their own frontier models likely means they are concerned about the pace of innovation by folks like anthropic and the fractured relationship with OpenAI (their are rumors microsoft might sue over the AWS/OpenAI deal). 

Cursor looking to raise more $$s at a crazy valuation while clearly falling behind in mindshare, with claude code and openai codex now the cool kids. They have work to do to stay relevant, especially as the turtles like jetbrains are starting to make progress in remaining relevant. It feels like the frontier labs are going to push to dominate the software development space, given its demonstrating the the clearest value at the moment and can fund their other investments. Cursor and Replit and few others are well funded so can probably survive and make a niche for themselves or get aquired at a full value. But its hard to see anyone other than Anthropic, OpenAI and Google being the dominant players in this space from where we currently stand. Even Microsoft's Github Copilot is struggling to stay relevent, though of course like much of Microsoft's Copilot efforts they benefit from the Microsoft leverage in the enterprise.

So things continue apace.
